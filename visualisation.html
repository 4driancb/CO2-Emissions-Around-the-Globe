Top 7 Highest CO2 Emitting Countries

The bar chart shows the average total CO2 emissions of different countries. The countries are sorted by their average total CO2 emissions in descending order. The top 7 countries with the highest average total CO2 emissions are: China, India, Russia, Japan, Germany, Ukraine, and Canada.
These countries are all major emitters of CO2, due to a variety of factors, including their large populations, industrialized economies, and reliance on fossil fuels. China is the world's largest emitter of CO2, accounting for over 27% of global emissions. This is due to its large population and rapid economic growth, which has led to a significant increase in energy consumption. India is the world's second-largest emitter of CO2, accounting for over 6% of global emissions. India's emissions are expected to continue to grow in the coming years as its economy develops. Russia is the world's fourth-largest emitter of CO2, accounting for over 4% of global emissions. Russia's emissions are primarily due to its large industrial sector and its reliance on fossil fuels for energy.

Emission Trends

The line chart shows the average total CO2 emissions of Canada, China, Germany, India, Japan, Russia, Ukraine, and the United Kingdom from 1970 to 2020. The x-axis of the chart shows the year, and the y-axis shows the average total CO2 emissions in metric tons per capita. The line chart shows that the average total CO2 emissions of all of the countries in the chart have increased over time. Each country can be highlighted by clicking on the legends.
