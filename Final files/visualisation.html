CO2 Emissions

Total CO2 Emissions Around The World

This world map shows how each country's CO2 emission per capita compare to each other using color saturation. There is a large difference in CO2 emissions between developed and developing countries. Developed countries generally have much higher CO2 emissions per capita than developing countries.

Total CO2 Emissions Around The World

The map shows that global action is needed to reduce CO2 emissions. Countries with high CO2 emissions need to take action in reducing their carbon emissions, and start moving to clean energy. That way, we can make our world healthier, and in turn increase the life span of living creatures.

Highest CO2 Emitting Countries

The bar chart shows the average CO2 emissions of some countries. They are sorted in descending order by their average total CO2 emissions from 1940 to 2021. Out of the entire list of countries, the top seven countries with the highest total CO2 emissions are China, Russia, Germany, Japan, Canada, Ukraine, and India.

These countries are all major emitters of CO2 due to a variety of factors, including their large populations, industrialised economies, and reliance on fossil fuels. China is the world's largest emitter of CO2, accounting for over 27% of global emissions. This is because they have a large population and rapid economic growth, which has led to a significant increase in energy consumption. Russia ranks second in CO2 emission. Russia's emissions are expected to grow continuously in the coming years as its nuclear power is developing. Both of these countries emit more CO2 than average.

Emission Trends

The interactive multiple line chart shows the average total CO2 emissions of Canada, China, Germany, India, Japan, Russia, and Ukraine from 1970 to 2020. The line chart shows that the average total CO2 emissions of all countries in the chart have increased over time.

The increase in CO2 emissions is mainly because of industrial development where more technologies and machines are created. This means that high amounts of energy are needed to produce them. Eventhough technological advancement is good for the world, we also need to improve our emission management, and prioritise using renewable energy.

Effect on Global Temperature

Land-Ocean Temperatures

The regression model between year and land-ocean temperature can be used to make estimates about future land-ocean temperature trends. The overall trend of the graph is a positive linear trend where land-ocean temperatures are expected to continuously rise in the upcoming years. However, it is important to note that this model is based on data from the past and does not take other factors into consideration. This model is derived mathematically and provides a numerical estimate.

Eventhough it is just an estimate, it is still important to realise that our earth is getting overall warmer. This leads to multiple negative impacts such as rising sea levels, extreme rainfall, hotter days, and many more (NSW gov, 2023).

Sea Ice

This area chart shows the average sea ice extent in the Artic monthly from 1980 to 2023, divided into four year ranges: 1980-1989, 1990-1999, 2000-2009, and 2010-2023. We can see that the more recent years have less ice cap extents, this is a result of increase in global temperature.

This decline in sea ice extent is a concern because it has a high contribution to climate change. Sea ice extent is important since it reflects sunlight back into space, which keeps the Earth cool. If there are less sea ice extent, there will be less sunlight reflected and the Earth warms as it is absorbed by the ocean.

We can also see that earlier years have good recovery in sea ice extents as it approaches colder seasons. Meanwhile, more recent years have less sea ice extent restorations during winter.

Effect on Humans

Death by Risk Factors

This bar chart shows how significant each risk factor is compared to each other in a ranked bar chart format, where the top most bar is the most significant cause of death. In 2019, air pollution is the third leading cause of death globally. The previous years have also shown its significance where it is ranked second and third until 2015.

CO2 emissions are a big contributor to air pollution. It is emitted from a variety of sources, including vehicles, power plants, and factories. Furthermore, it reacts with other pollutants that form harmful smog that affects human health adversely.

There are a lot of things we can do to reduce CO2 emissions and improve our health. These include switching to renewable energy soruces, using public transport instead of personal vehicles, and recycling.
